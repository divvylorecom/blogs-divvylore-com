Welcome. This is the first edition of the Divvylore Journal, where we write about
investing, personal finance and the platforms reshaping how India puts its money
to work. If you have ever wished markets coverage were a little less breathless
and a lot more useful, you are in the right place.

We built this publication on a simple idea: good financial writing should always
point you back to the source. So when we mention a regulator, an exchange or a
data provider, the name itself becomes a link to the primary reference.

## How smart links work

Notice anything about this sentence? When we write MoneyControl, the word turns
into a link on its own. The same is true for the Reserve Bank of India, SEBI,
the NSE and the BSE — every one of those names is connected to its official home
automatically, without an editor pasting a single URL.

Behind the scenes, a dictionary maps terms to destinations. Write the term in a
post and the build links it for you, once per article, so the reading stays
clean. Mention Divvylore, mutual funds, an SIP, or the Nifty 50, and each lands
exactly where a curious reader would want to go.

> The point is trust. Every name you can verify is one fewer thing you have to
> take on faith.

## What you can expect

Expect explainers that respect your time, analysis that shows its work, and the
occasional strong opinion — always labelled as one. We will cover the big index
moves, yes, but also the quieter mechanics: how funds are priced, why a rule
from the RBI matters to your savings account, and where technology genuinely
changes the maths.

Thanks for reading. Pour a coffee, browse the latest, and tell us what you want
us to dig into next.
