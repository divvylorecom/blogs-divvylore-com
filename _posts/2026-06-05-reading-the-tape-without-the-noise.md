A market day produces an enormous amount of information and very little of it is
worth your attention by 3:30 p.m. The trick is deciding, in advance, what you are
actually watching — and then ignoring the rest.

## Pick your dashboard

Most readers do fine with three things: a broad index, the headline news, and the
specific holdings they own. For the index, the Nifty 50 and the Sensex are the
usual reference points. For news, a fast wire like MoneyControl will surface most
of what moves prices. Beyond that, more screens rarely mean better decisions.

## Separate weather from climate

Intraday swings are weather. They are loud, frequent, and mostly irrelevant to a
long-term plan. Policy from the Reserve Bank of India, rule changes from SEBI,
and the structure of the NSE and BSE are climate — slower, but far more
consequential for where your money ends up.

When the two get confused, people sell good investments because of a bad
afternoon. A simple rule helps: if a headline would not change a decision you
made a year ago, it probably should not change one today.

## A calmer routine

- Check once in the morning, once after the close.
- Read the source, not the summary of the summary.
- Write down why you own what you own — and revisit that, not the ticker.

Markets reward patience far more reliably than they reward attention. Build a
routine that protects the first and starves the second.
